Cross-checking our Polarization Angles

We've come up with a procedure to cross-check that the RPS-derived polarization angles likely correct without unblinding ourselves to the birefrinence angle. My RPS measurements indicate that the the tile-to-tile spread in pair-difference polarization angle is ~1°. That means that I should be able to split our coadded maps into subsets based on spread we expect from our RPS-derived polarization angles and then compare the difference in angle among the different subsets. Since we're looking only at relative differences, we keep ourselves blind to the absolute birefringence angle.

The limiting factor to this technique is going to be the increased uncertainty on α due to decreasing the detector count per-map. So before I do anything with the real data, I want to know beforehand whether of not this will actually work by looking first at the subsets created from the 10 B2018 sims I made.

Conclusions

By fitting rotation angles to our standard suite of sims, we now have answers to the questions we asked in §1:

  1. Does the global rotation fitting function impart a bias on α?
    • No, all angles are consistent with zero.
  2. What is the expected statistical uncertainties on α?
    • I quote the baseline statistical uncertainty from the 1-σ standard deviation of the matrix-purified Lensed-LCDM+Noise+Dust distribution: 0.08°.
  3. Can we accurately capture the statistics on α with fewer than 499 sims?
    • Yes, the difference between the 499 sims and 10 is, at most, ~0.01° which is satisfactory.
  4. What happens when we use our B18 Purification matrix on smaller subsets of data?
    • The variance on α decreases by at least a factor of 1.5 without any apparent negative side-effects.

Next Steps

An immediate follow-up to this posting will be exploring the ability of low-N simsets to perform independent cross-checks of RPS-derived polarization angles.

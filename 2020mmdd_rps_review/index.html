Introduction

Motivation

  1. Adding a Chern-Simons term to the EM Lagrangian results in Uniform Cosmic Birefringence
  2. Means: the speed of circularly polarized light changes depending on pol. direction
  3. For linearly pol. light, polarization orientation rotates as it propogates
  4. We'll see larger rotation $\alpha$ the longer it propagates
  5. CMB is perfect for this sort of observation.
  6. $\alpha$ is degenerate with instrumental rotation $\Delta \phi$
  7. Constraining $\alpha$ requires calibrating the instrument rotation.
  8. Rich history of constraints on UCB, but we think we can do better (Table 1).

Setup, Observations, and Dataset

Setup

The RPS is deployed atop a 12m mast on the Martin A. Pomerantz Observatory and observed by BICEP3 in the Dark Sector Laboratory 200m away. Even atop the mast, the RPS is well below the normal elevation range so a beam-filling aluminum honeycomb flat mirror is installed with $45^\circ$ tilt to redirect rays onto the horizon when that telescope is pointed at zenith.

The RPS is electrically chopped at 20Hz.

Observations

We conduct observations by rastering across the source $9^\circ$ in azimuth and $2^\circ$ in elevation with $0.1^\circ$ steps in elevation. The source is fixed at a single command angle per-raster and a full RPS observation consists of 13 separate rasters where the RPS is commanded from $-180^\circ$ to $180^\circ$ in $30^\circ$ increments. As the RPS rotates through it command angles, the observed amplitude changes as a function of the coupling the source polarization axis and a detector's polarization axis. We call this a modulation curve Fig. 2.3.

Rasters are have very high signal-to-noise with a median SNR of $\sim6000$ at maximum amplitude.

Dataset

The RPS observations used in this analysis were taken in January and February of 2018. Five observations were taken over four boresight angles of $1.25^\circ$, $46.25^\circ$, $91.25^\circ$, $136.25^\circ$ with and extra observation taken at $46.25^\circ$. The BICEP3 focal plane is clocked $1.25^\circ$ WRT the $DK=0^\circ$ and so we add $1.25^\circ$ to ensure maximum polarization coupling to the source. Observations over multiple boresight angles provides a consistency check of our measurement repeatability.

Analysis

2D Gaussian Fits

We fit a 2-D Gaussian profile across all beams in a rasterset simultaneously where the beam amplitude is a free parameter for each raster but the beam center, beam widths, and correlation coefficient are single parameters fit across the whole rasterset. This is to mitigate bad fits when the RPS and detector are 90° out of phase and the constraining power on the beam is poor.

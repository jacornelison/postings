Subsection 1

Our model for the modulation curve amplitude, $A$, as a function of RPS rotation angle, $\theta$, is:

\begin{equation} A(\theta) = G\left(\cos\left(2\left(\theta+\psi\right)\right)-\frac{\epsilon+1}{\epsilon-1}\right)\left(N_1\cos(\theta)+N_2\sin(\theta)+1\right) \end{equation} where:
  • $G$ is the gain of a detector
  • $\phi$ is the polarization angle WRT to the RPS wire grid (The wire grid pol response is align to zenith)
  • $\epsilon$ is the cross-polar {response,(in)efficiency, leakage}
  • $N1,\;N2$ are nuisance parameters which account for miscollimation of the RPS axis of rotation.
Together with our data, $A_d$, the chi-square we minimize is: \begin{equation} \chi^2 = \sum\left(w(\theta)\left[A_d(\theta)-A(\theta)\right]\right)^2 \end{equation}

where $w$ is a weighting we could apply to the residuals.

I chose three different weighting options:

  • Uniform: \begin{equation} w(\theta) = 1 \end{equation} This is what we currently use in our RPS analysis.
  • Threshold: \begin{equation} w(\theta) = \begin{cases} 1 & \; A_d(\theta) < A_{thresh}\\ 0 & \; A_d(\theta) \ge A_{thresh}\\ \end{cases} \end{equation} This puts all of the weight on the lower amplitudes where the constraining power on $\epsilon$ should be highest.
  • 1/Amp: \begin{equation} w(\theta) = {A_d(\theta)}^{-1}-\text{min}\left({A_d(\theta)}^{-1}\right) \end{equation} This weighting does the same as above, but avoids establishing an arbitrary threshold.

Simulations

I created simsets of 100 modulation curves at DK-angles of 0° to 180° in 10° increments. Modulation curves are created using Eq. 1 with $\theta = [-180:30:180]$ to match RPS observations. For my fiducial input parameters, I used the following: \begin{equation} P = [\phi\; \epsilon\; G\; N_1\; N_2] = [0\;0.03 \;0.5 \;0 \;0] \end{equation} I chose an $\epsilon=0.03$ mostly to ensure we've got a sufficiently high SNR measurement of it, but otherwise this choice was pretty arbitrary. Because amplitude-scaling noise is the dominating reason, I include a 5% Gaussian noise that scales with amplitude in these sims to match roughly what we see in the data: \begin{equation} A_d = A(\theta|P)+A(\theta|P)\times\,N(\mu=0,\sigma=0.05) \end{equation} I should also note that each weighting option at each DK angle uses the same 100 noise realizations. I fit this modulation curves to the same model in Eq.1 using Matlab's lsqnonlin to minimize our chi-square in Eq. 2.

The pager below shows histograms of the best-fit parameters for $\phi$ and $\epsilon$ at various DK angles.

We can see that for $\epsilon$, the scatter is consistently better for the Threshold and 1/Amp weighting schemes. Interestingly, the scatter for $\phi$ is also fairly sensitive to the amplitude scaling noise which is something I was surprised by -- as I was expecting that the constraining power from the shape of the sine wave would dominate over the amplitude-scaling noise. Also, it's not really clear why $\phi$ is so poorly constrained at multiples of 30°, though I suspect is might have something to do with my implementation.

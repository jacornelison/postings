Comparing subsets of 2022 RPS data

The pager below shows scatter plots and difference histograms of polarization angles estimated from the 2022 RPS campaign which includes both Type 5 and Type 11 schedules. These angles were estimated using one value for each of the mirror and source parameters. The mirror orientation was derived from the Moon observations. The source location was derived using the Moon-derived mirror parameters and fitting over all type-5 observations only. We have redundant observations at DK=68 with type 5 schedules, DK=0 with type 11 schedules, and an observation at DK=90 for both types 5 and 11 which are included in the pager. The last selection 'All-DK split' is where I split the all of observations into to subsets roughly evenly distributed across DK angles and schedules types: 'AVG1' averages over DKs(type) of -81(T5), -23(T11), 0(T11), 45(T5), 68(T5), 90(T5), 174(T5) ; and 'AVG2' averages -68(T11), 0(T11), 23(T5), 61(T5), 68(T5), 90(T11), 135(T11).
